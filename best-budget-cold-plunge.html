The premium chiller tubs get all the attention, but here's the truth: you can get 90% of the benefit of cold plunging for less than 5% of the price. Cold water is cold water. If you're willing to add your own ice, a budget tub does the job just fine — and it's the smartest way to start.

Here are the best budget cold plunges under $500 in 2026, plus exactly what you give up compared to the expensive models.

What you give up (and what you don't)

Going budget means giving up exactly one thing: automatic chilling. That's it. With a budget tub you'll add ice manually — usually two to four bags for a proper session, or you can freeze large reusable ice blocks to cut the cost.

What you don't give up is the actual cold exposure or its benefits. The water in a $150 tub and a $5,000 tub feels identical at the same temperature. The premium price buys convenience, filtration and looks — not a better plunge.

Smart path: Buy a budget tub, plunge for 30 days, and see if it sticks. If it becomes a daily habit and the ice runs get annoying, then upgrade to a chiller tub. You'll have lost almost nothing testing it.
